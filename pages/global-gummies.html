Global Gummies: A World of Texture

The gummy bear may have started it all, but the world of gummy candy has expanded far beyond the simple gelatin bear. Today, we are witnessing a global exchange of textures and flavors that is revolutionizing the gummy aisle.

Japan has long been a pioneer in this space. Brands like Kasugai and Cororo challenged the western idea of a "gummy" by introducing textures that mimic real fruit—soft, bursting with juice, and sometimes encased in a thin skin that snaps when you bite it. The "peelable" gummy trend is the latest viral sensation, offering an interactive experience where you peel a gummy fruit just like the real thing.

Texture as a Flavor

In the West, gummies were traditionally chewy and bouncy. In Scandinavia, salty licorice gummies offer a firm, resistant bite. But the new wave is all about variety. We're seeing gummies with liquid centers, airy marshmallows foams, and gritty sugar coatings all in one bag.

"The future of candy isn't just about how it tastes, but how it feels."

This texture revolution is driven by curious consumers who travel the world through their taste buds. From the spicy, tamarind-coated gummies of Mexico to the floral, soft jellies of Turkey, the definition of what a gummy can be is stretching—literally.

So next time you reach for a pack of sweets, look for something that promises a new sensation. You might just find that the texture is the sweetest part.
