The Candy Cane Story

It's the symbol of the winter holidays, adorning trees and filling stockings. But the candy cane wasn't always the striped, hooked, peppermint stick we know today.

The Legend of the Choirmaster

The most popular origin story dates back to 1670 in Cologne, Germany. A choirmaster at the Cologne Cathedral, seeking a way to keep the children quiet during the long Living Creation services, asked a local candy maker to produce sugar sticks.

"The distinctive 'J' shape was added to represent a shepherd's crook, or perhaps the 'J' for Jesus."

Originally, these sticks were pure white and not flavored with peppermint. The famous red stripes and mint flavor didn't appear until the early 20th century in America. The 1920s saw the invention of the machine that could automatically twist the candy, allowing for mass production.

A Sticky Tradition

Despite modern innovations like blueberry or bacon-flavored canes, the classic peppermint remains King. It's a reminder of how food, religion, and technology can intertwine to create a tradition that feels timeless.

Whether you eat the hook first or the straight end, you are participating in a 350-year-old ritual of sweet silence.
