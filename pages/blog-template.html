The History of Nerds: A Crunchy Legacy

It was 1983 when the candy world was forever changed by the introduction of a tiny, crunchy, irregularly shaped sensation: Nerds.

Created by the Willy Wonka Candy Factory, Nerds were a departure from the smooth chocolates and chewy gummies that dominated the aisles. They were pure sugar, tangy and sweet, coated in a carnauba wax glaze that gave them their signature crunch.

Two Flavors, One Box

One of the most innovative aspects of Nerds was the packaging. The split box allowed two different flavors to coexist without mixing. Strawberry and Grape became the definitive duo, a partnership as iconic as peanut butter and jelly.

"Reinventing the way we interact with candy, one box at a time."

The name "Nerds" itself is a bit of a mystery. Some say it came from a Dr. Seuss book, "If I Ran the Zoo," which mentions a creature called a Nerd. Others believe it was just a reflection of the 80s slang. Whatever the origin, it stuck.

Today, Nerds have evolved. From Nerds Ropes to Big Chewy Nerds, the brand continues to innovate. But for true connoisseurs, nothing beats pouring a handful of those original, tiny, crunchy rocks straight into your mouth.
